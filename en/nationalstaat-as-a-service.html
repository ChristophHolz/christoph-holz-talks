Nation state as a service

What does Facebook still lack to be a real state? Estonia already has it.

On the journey through cyberspace, your Google ID matters far more than your passport. Facebook has a police force. Computer games have a currency. We usually only know all that from nation-states. Nigeria's central bank has a cryptocurrency. Estonia has a fully automated, impartial, citizen-friendly online bureaucracy. Australia is listed in a corporate register. That things won't stay the way they are is obvious. To figure out where we want to go, we first need to know what's actually going on.

Three Thoughts from the Talk

Digitalization is the first revolution that isn't about seizing power, but about abolishing it.

It is the supreme feat of political manipulation to steer our surveillance skepticism toward corporations—and thereby away from the state, whose true instrument of power it is.

Why do we actually behave even when no one's watching? Because someone is watching after all. God sees everything and hears everything – the so-called invisible observer. But Nietzsche says God is dead. No matter. We now have the NSA, government trojans and data retention. As long as we behave.
