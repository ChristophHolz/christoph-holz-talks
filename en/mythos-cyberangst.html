The Myth of Cyber Fear

What's real about the dangers of the internet — and what's marketing theater

"If hackers can supposedly break in anywhere, anytime, easy as pie, then why is there still money in my account? Why does the economy work when everything could be shut down at any moment? Something doesn't add up. The truth is less spectacular: fear is a business model. Christoph Holz separates real danger from marketing theater — from total surveillance, which technically doesn't even work, to the mundane risk that actually counts: the human, the weak password, the update that was never installed. A calm, optimistic look at cybersecurity. Because whoever separates theater from danger acts effectively instead of in a panic. Whoever understands how, wins."

Three Thoughts from the Talk

Inflating online sabotage into an uncontrollable cyberwar is the best excuse incompetent system administrators have ever had.

Mass surveillance seems plausible to us because it meets the criteria of a conspiracy theory.

Digital mass surveillance is an unsolvable problem of computer science. At best, you can catch stupid terrorists with it. That only raises the IQ of the remaining population. Even though the relevant US programs have failed, our belief in surveillance helps us behave. The legend of mass surveillance is a disciplinary measure.
